Wearables are very popular these days, but when someone thinks of wearables, they mostly think of the watches and the glasses. Although, there are a lot of different types of wearables. In this research I focus on the wearables that are not glasses and watches.

Project UnderSkin

To go to the absolute opposite of the glasses and watches, there are wearables that can be worn under the skin. It is a concept from the designers of Fitbit but has not been made yet. They thought of a tattoo that will be placed under the skin to track the activity of everything you touch, with that hand. It can be used to open locks, pay and might show something when you touch someone else. It tracks location and movement, therefore it can not only track, but also contextualize gestures you make.

Here's a link to a video about Project UnderSkin.

Athos

Another non-controversial wearable has to be worn on a bare skin, it is a wearable spandex. It can be worn while working out. The spandex translates your movements into meanings. It gives feedback of the work out, it can be used as a personal trainer. You can say how much of a burn you want in your muscles and it helps you get the technique right. It was founded by Dhananja Jayalath and Christopher Wiebe, when they went to the gym together, it was always and competition, this is how they came to the idea. It is not only underwear, but also shirts and sport bras. This way the sensors can track all the muscles.
