The first lecture was about Exertion Gaming, a type of gaming where the user needs to use his whole body in order to communicate with the device. After this lecture we got an assignment, in this assignment we have to come up with a new concept (for an exertion interface) to make students and employees of THUAS move within the main THUAS building. Underneath I will display my concept for this assignment.

Around the clock

My idea is based on the movie The Hunger Games: Catching Fire, where there are 12 different obstacles, based on the time. In the THUAS, we have eight different floors, which means there can be eight different themes, I worked out two themes, so the idea is clear.

There will be eight different themes for the different. Above you can see one floor with water, with sharks. The students and employees need to walk on the wooden blocks to not get eaten by the sharks.

On the second sketch you can see that there are holes displayed on the ground. When someone steps in a hole there will be a sound effect of someone falling and screaming.

So, I told there will be eight themes, the first two are shown in the sketches, but then there are still six themes left. Those themes are not visualized.

Ideas for themes to use for exertion gaming floors

  • Snakes and insects.
  • Quicksand.
  • Glass bridge that cracks.
  • Bridge with wooden pallets that move/shake.
  • Lava where you have to step on stones to not get burnt.

This is the end of the first assignment, coming up with a new concept of exertion gaming in the THUAS.
