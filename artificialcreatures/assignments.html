Artificial creatures are electronics that have human like behavior, for example a robot vacuum, that “walks” through the room and stops when the room is clean. During the lecture the homeostasis model was explained to us, this shows external factors that influence the internal state of the machine. For the vacuum those are dust, energy and safety.

The printer as an artificial creature

For the exercise, I’m going to explain those factors for a printer, that a printer is also an artificial creature. We start by setting up the homeostasis model, which includes ink, electricity, paper and orders. The printer wants a lot of ink, electricity and paper, so it can keep working, but it wants no orders, that is why it always works those orders.

Printing influences the factors of ink, paper and orders, because those are used when something needs to be printed. Therefore, it goes down, which is negative, because when this happens often, it can’t print out orders anymore. Orders go down when whole documents are printed, but as opposed to the ink and the paper, this is a positive thing, because that is the purpose of the printer, to print the orders. For the electricity, printers usually work with a plug, which means it is full of energy or it has no energy at all.

For those reasons there can only be two states for the electricity, but all different kind of states for the paper, ink and orders. The paper and ink have a certain maximum, there can’t be put in more paper than that maximum, the same goes for the ink. But the orders can have a different maximum, sometimes there will only be one order, and sometimes there will be dozens, therefore, the stat can be full and only have one order, but it can also be half full, for ten orders.
